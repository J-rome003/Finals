Borrowing cash terms" typically refer to the specific conditions, rules, and agreements established between a borrower and a lender when borrowing money. These terms can vary based on the lending institution, type of loan, and the agreement made between the parties involved. Common borrowing cash terms include:

1.Loan Amount: The total amount of money borrowed.

2.Interest Rate: The percentage of the loan amount that the borrower needs to pay as interest over the loan term.

3.Loan Term: The period within which the borrowed amount, along with interest, needs to be repaid.

4.Repayment Schedule: The agreed-upon timetable for making payments, whether monthly, quarterly, annually, etc.

5.Collateral: If applicable, the asset or property provided by the borrower as security for the loan. It can be seized by the lender if the borrower fails to repay the loan.

6.Grace Period: The time period after the due date during which a payment can be made without incurring late fees.

7.Late Payment Fees: The charges imposed on the borrower if a payment is not made within the specified grace period.

8.Prepayment Terms: The conditions under which the borrower can pay off the loan before the scheduled term without incurring penalties.

9.Default: Circumstances or actions that constitute a breach of the loan agreement, leading to serious consequences, such as legal action or seizure of collateral.

10.Origination Fee: A fee charged by the lender for processing the loan application.

11.Credit Score: The borrower's creditworthiness, often influencing the interest rate and loan approval.

12.Terms and Conditions: The detailed rules and regulations governing the loan agreement, outlining the rights and responsibilities of both the borrower and the lender.

13.These terms can vary widely based on the type of loan (personal loan, mortgage, business loan, etc.) and the policies of the lending institution. It's crucial for borrowers to thoroughly understand these terms before agreeing to a loan to ensure they are aware of their financial obligations and rights.
